European influence and colonialism began in the 15th century, when the Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama discovered the sea route from Europe to India.
The modern city of Casablanca was founded by Berber fishermen in the 10th century BC. The Phoenicians, Romans and Maronites used it as a strategic port called Anfax.
The Portuguese destroyed it and rebuilt it as Casa Branca, but abandoned it after an earthquake in 1755.
The city was rebuilt by the Moroccan sultan Daru I-Badyana and the Spanish merchants, who established trading centres there, named it Casablanca.
The capital of Moldova is Chişinău. The local language is Romanian, but Russian is also widely used.
Moldova is a multi-ethnic republic that has suffered from ethnic conflict.
In 1994, this conflict led to the creation of the self-proclaimed Republic of Transnistria in eastern Moldova, which has its own government and currency but is not recognised by any UN member state.
Economic links have been re-established between the two parts of Moldova despite the failure of political negotiations.
Moldova's main religion is Orthodox Christianity.
