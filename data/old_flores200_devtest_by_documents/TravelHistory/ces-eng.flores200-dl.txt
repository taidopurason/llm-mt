European influence and colonialism began in the 15th century when the Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama discovered the sea route (Cape Route) from Europe to India.
The modern city of Casablanca was founded by Berber fishermen in the 10th century BC and was used by the Phoenicians, Romans and Merenids as a strategic port called Anfa.
The Portuguese destroyed and rebuilt the city under the name Casa Branca, and abandoned it after the 1755 earthquake.
The Sultan of Morocco rebuilt the town as Dara l-Badya and the Spanish merchants who established trading bases there gave it the name Casablanca.
The capital of Moldova is Chisinau. The local language is Romanian, but Russian is also widely spoken.
Moldova is a multi-ethnic republic that has been plagued by various ethnic conflicts.
This conflict led in 1994 to the creation of the self-proclaimed Transnistrian Moldovan Republic in eastern Moldova, which has its own government and currency but is not recognised by any UN member state.
Economic ties between the two parts of Moldova have been restored despite the failure of political negotiations.
The main religion of Moldova is Orthodox Christianity.
