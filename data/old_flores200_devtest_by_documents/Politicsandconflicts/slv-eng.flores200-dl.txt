Among the 1 400 respondents to the 2010 federal election, the number of those opposed to Australia becoming a republic has increased by 8% since 2008.
During the 2010 federal election campaign, the Prime Minister of the transitional government, Julia Gillard, argued that she believed Australia should become a republic after the end of Queen Elizabeth II's reign.
34% of respondents to the poll agreed with the demand that Queen Elizabeth II should be the last ruler of Australia.
On the one hand, 29% of respondents think that Australia should become a republic as soon as possible, while on the other hand, 31% think that Australia should not become a republic at all.
