The use of video recordings has led to important breakthroughs in the interpretation of micromovements - facial movements lasting only a few microseconds.
Specifically, you can tell if the other person is lying to you by correctly interpreting microexpressions.
In his article The President's Speech, Oliver Sacks suggested how people who are unable to understand speech due to brain damage are still able to accurately judge sincerity.
He even suggests that such abilities to interpret human behavior may be shared by animals such as domestic dogs.
