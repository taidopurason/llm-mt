Video recording made important discoveries in explaining microstrokes, facial movements that last only a few milliseconds.
In particular, they say that the correct interpretation of microexpressions should make it possible to detect whether a person is lying.
In his article The President's Speech, Oliver Sacks explained that people who cannot understand speech due to brain injury are nonetheless able to accurately assess sincerity.
It even assumes that animals such as domestic dogs also possess such interpreting abilities in human behavior.
