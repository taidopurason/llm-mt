During this period of European history, the Catholic Church, which had become rich and voracious, began to be carefully controlled.
For over a thousand years, the Christian religion had united European countries, despite the differences between their languages and customs.
Its all-encompassing power affected everyone, from the king to the common citizen.
One of the main beliefs of Christians is that wealth should be used to alleviate suffering and poverty, and church funds are intended precisely for this purpose.
The center of ecclesiastical power had been in Rome for over a thousand years, and this concentration of power and money raised the question in many whether this principle was adhered to.
