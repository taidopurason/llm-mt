It was during this period in European history that the Catholic Church, which had become rich and powerful, came under close scrutiny.
For more than a thousand years, the Christian religion had united the countries of Europe, despite the differences between their languages and customs.
Its all-encompassing power influenced everyone, from the king to the common man.
One of the fundamental beliefs of Christians is that wealth should be used to alleviate suffering and poverty, and the Church's financial resources are intended for this very purpose.
The centre of ecclesiastical power had been in Rome for over a thousand years, and this concentration of power and money led many to question whether this principle was being respected.
