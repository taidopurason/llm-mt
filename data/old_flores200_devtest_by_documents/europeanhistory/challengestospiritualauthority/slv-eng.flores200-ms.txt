During this period of European history, the Catholic Church came under scrutiny, enriching and expanding its authority.
The Christian faith has connected European countries for more than a millennium, despite differences in language and customs. I
Its ubiquitous power influenced both kings and ordinary people.
One of the main Christian doctrines is that wealth should be used to alleviate suffering and poverty, and that the money of the church is intended to do just that.
The seat of the central authority of the church has been based in Rome for over a thousand years, and this concentration of power and money has led many to doubt whether it really follows this principle.
