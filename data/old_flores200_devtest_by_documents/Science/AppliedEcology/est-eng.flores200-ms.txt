Nature tourism attracts people who are interested in visiting natural places to enjoy the views as well as wild plants and animals.
Examples of on-site activities include hunting, fishing, photography, birdwatching and visiting parks, as well as learning about the ecosystem.
An example would be visiting the Boreno orangutans, taking pictures and learning about them.
Every morning, people leave small towns by car to go to work, and they, in turn, are bypassed by others, whose place of work is where they just left.
In this dynamic transport shuttle, everyone is in some way connected to and supported by a transport system based on private vehicles.
Science has now shown that this huge carbon dioxide-producing economy has pushed the biosphere out of place from one of its stable states, which has been favorable to human evolution over the past 2 million.
