Many observable rhythms of physiology and behaviour often depend on endogenous cycles and their creation by biological clocks.
The occurrence of periodic rhythms, which are not simply a response to external periodic factors, has been documented in most living things, including bacteria, fungi, plants and animals.
The biological clock is a self-sustaining oscillatory system that continues to run in free-running cycles even in the absence of external influences.
