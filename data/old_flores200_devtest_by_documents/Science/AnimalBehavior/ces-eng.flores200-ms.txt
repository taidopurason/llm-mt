Many recorded rhythms in physiology and behavior often depend fundamentally on the presence of endogenous cycles and their production through the biological clock.
Periodic rhythms, which are not just responses to external periodic stimuli, have been documented in most living beings, including bacteria, fungi, plants, and animals.
The biological clock is a self-sufficient oscillator that continues in the time of a free-flowing cycle even without the presence of external stimuli.
