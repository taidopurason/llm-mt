After the adoption of Congress on July 4, a handwritten proposal was signed by Congress chairman John Hancock and Secretary Charles Thomson, sent a few blocks further to John Dunlap.
Overnight, something was made between 150 and 200 copies, which are currently called "Dunlap prints".
For the first time, the document was publicly read by John Nixon on July 8 in the courtyard of the Hall of Independence.
One was sent to Georgi Washington on July 6, who read him to his soldiers in New York on July 9th.One of the copies came to London on 10 August.
The oldest known preserved document specimens are the so -called Dunlap Broadsides, or a total of 25 copies from Dunlap's printer.The original manuscript has not been preserved.
