For example, the most common photo format in the world is 35mm, which was the most common film size at the height of the analog film era.
They are still produced today, but more importantly, their aspect ratio has been adopted by the formats of digital camera sensors.
Although somewhat confusing, the 35mm format actually has a width of 36mm and a height of 24mm.
Thus, the aspect ratio of this format (divided by twelve to obtain the simplest ratio in integers) is 3:2.
Many common formats (such as the APS family of formats) equal to or close to this aspect ratio.
