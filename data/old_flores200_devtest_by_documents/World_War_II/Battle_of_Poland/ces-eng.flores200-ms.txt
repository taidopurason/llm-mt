By September 17, 1939, the Polish defenses had already been breached, and the only hope was a retreat and regrouping of forces along the Romanian bridgehead.
However, these plans fell through almost overnight when more than 800,000 soldiers of the Soviet Red Army formed the Belarusian and Ukrainian fronts after the troops invaded the eastern regions of Poland, violating the Riga Peace Treaty, the Soviet-Polish Non-Aggression Pact and other international treaties, both bilateral and multilateral.
