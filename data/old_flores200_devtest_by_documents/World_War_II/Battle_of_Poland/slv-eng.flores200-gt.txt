Until September 17, 1939, the Polish defense had already been broken, and the only hope was withdrawal and redistribution along the Romanian bridge.
These plans, however, became outdated virtually overnight, when 800,000 soldiers of the Red Army of the Soviet Union ran and established the Belarusian and Ukrainian fronts after the occupation of the East Polish regions, which was contrary to the peace treaty from Riga, the Soviet-Polish-Polish non-collection pact and othersinternational treaties, both bilateral and multilateral.
