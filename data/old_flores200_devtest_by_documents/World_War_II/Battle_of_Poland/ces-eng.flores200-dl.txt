On 17 September 1939 the Polish defences had already been breached, and the only hope was to retreat and regroup along the Romanian bridgehead.
However, these plans took a turn for the worse almost overnight when more than 800,000 Soviet Red Army troops formed the Belorussian and Ukrainian fronts after the troops invaded the eastern regions of Poland in violation of the Treaty of Riga, the Soviet-Polish Non-Aggression Pact, and other international treaties, both bilateral and multilateral.
