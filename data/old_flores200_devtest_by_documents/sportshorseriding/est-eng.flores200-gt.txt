Footwear hanging on each side of the saddle is designed to support the passenger's feet.
They help the rider to balance better, but can be dangerous as the rider's feet can get stuck.
If the rider is thrown off the horse, but his leg is stuck in the footer, it may happen that the horse can take him to run away.Precautions should be taken to reduce this threat.
First of all, most riders wear riding boots with a fairly narrow soles.
Next, some saddles, especially English saddles, have protective barriers that allow the strap to come off the saddle when the fallen rider pulls it backwards.
