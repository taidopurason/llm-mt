Today in the Atlantic Ocean formed the tenth named storm of the season of Atlantic hurricanes, the subtropical storm of Jerry.
The National Hurricane Center (NHC) claims that Jerry is not at the moment of any threat to the mainland.
The US Technical Corps estimates that 15 cm of rainfall could lead to a rupture of already damaged dams.
Ninth Ward, which was flooded with a height of 20 feet during the hurricane, has now has water over the belt of an adult because the nearby flood dam overflows.
Through the dam, water overflows on a section of 30 meters wide.
