Red tides are caused by unusually high concentrations of Karenia brevis, a marine unicellular organism that occurs in nature.
The coincidence of natural factors can create ideal conditions that enable a drastic increase in the number of algae.
This algae produces a neurotoxin that can numb the nerves of both humans and fish.
Due to high concentrations of poison in the waters, fish often die.
Humans can be affected by inhaling contaminated water that is carried into the air by winds and waves.
