A tornado is a column of very low-pressure rotating air that sucks the surrounding air in and upwards.
The tornado generates strong winds (often 100-200 mph) and can lift heavy objects into the air, carrying them along its path.
They originate as funnels from storm clouds and become tornadoes when they touch land.
