A tornado is a swirling tower of ultra-low air pressure that pulls the surrounding air into itself and up.
They create strong winds (often 100-200 miles/hour) and can lift heavy objects and carry them in the direction of movement of the tornado.
Initially, they look like funnels descending from storm clouds, and upon contact with the ground, they develop into "tornadoes".
