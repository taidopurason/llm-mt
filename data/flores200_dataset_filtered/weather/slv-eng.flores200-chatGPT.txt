Felicia, which was once classified as a Category 4 hurricane on the Saffir-Simpson scale, weakened into a tropical depression, and then dissipated on Tuesday.
It left rains across almost all the islands, although there are no reports of material damage or floods for now.
Precipitations, which reached 6.34 inches on the gauge in Oahu, were described as "beneficial."
Some rains were accompanied by storms and frequent lightning.
A tornado is a swirling column of air with extremely low air pressure that pulls in and upward surrounding air.
They create strong winds (often 100-200 miles/hour) and can lift heavy objects and carry them in the direction of the tornado's movement.
At first, they appear as funnels descending from thunderclouds, but upon reaching the ground, they develop into "tornadoes."
