Red tide is caused by a higher concentration of Karenia brevis, a naturally occurring single-celled marine organism.
A potential combination of natural factors can create ideal conditions, due to which the occurrence of algae will increase significantly.
Algae produce a neurotoxin that can disable the nerves of both humans and fish.
Fish often die due to the high content of toxins in the water.
People can be affected by inhaling affected water, which is carried into the air by wind and waves.
