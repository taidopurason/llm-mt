The red tide is caused by unusually high concentrations of Karenia brevis, a marine unicellular organism that occurs naturally.
With the coincidence of natural factors, ideal conditions can be created that allow to drastically increase the number of algae.
These algae produce a neurotoxin that can numb nerves in both humans and fish.
Due to high concentrations of poison in waters, fish often die.
Humans can be affected by inhaled infected water carried into the air by winds and waves.
