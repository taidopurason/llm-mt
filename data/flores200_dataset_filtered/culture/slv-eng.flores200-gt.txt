The military nature of the Roman Empire contributed to faster medical progress.
Emperor Augustus began training doctors and they formed the first Roman medical corps dedicated to recovery after battles.
Surgeons were familiar with a variety of sedatives, including morphine from poppy seed extracts and scopolamine from black cohosh seeds.
They were trained in amputation to save patients from gangrene, and in the use of bandages and arterial clamps to stop the flow of blood.
Within a few centuries, the Roman Empire made great strides in the field of medicine and shaped most of the knowledge we know today.
