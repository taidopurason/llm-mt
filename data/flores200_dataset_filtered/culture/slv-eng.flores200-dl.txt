The military nature of the Roman Empire helped to speed up medical progress.
Emperor Augustus began training doctors and they formed the first Roman medical corps, dedicated to recovering from battles.
Surgeons were introduced to various sedatives, including morphine from poppy seed extracts and scopolamine from black locust seeds.
They were trained in amputation to save patients from gangrene and in the use of bandages and arterial clips to stop the flow of blood.
Within a few centuries, the Roman Empire had achieved great successes in the field of medicine and shaped much of the knowledge we know today.
