The militaristic nature of the Roman Empire contributed to the development of medical innovations.
Doctors began to be recruited by Emperor Augustus, and even the first Roman medical corps was formed, whose help was used to treat the injured in battle.
Surgeons were familiar with a variety of sedatives, including morphine derived from poppy seed extract and scopolamine obtained from dog-solstice seeds.
They became experts in rescuing patients with gangrene by amputation and stopping bleeding with tourniquets and blood vessel closures.
Over several centuries, the Roman Empire achieved great advances in the field of medicine and laid the foundation for much of our modern knowledge.
