The beginnings of Taiwan date back to the 15th century, when near the European sailors recorded the name of the island as Ilha Formosa, a beautiful island.
In 1624, the Dutch East Indian company creates a base in the southwestern Taiwan and initiates the transformation of the original practice of grain production, employs Chinese workers on its rice and sugar plantations.
In 1683 the units of the Qing Dynasty (1644–1912) took over the southwestern and north coasts of Taiwan and in 1885 Taiwan was declared the province of the Empire Qing.
In 1895, after a defeat in the first Sino-Japanese War (1894–1895), the Qing Government signed the Simonosek Mírov Treaty, on the basis of which it handed over the sovereignty over Taiwan Japan.This ruled this island until 1945.
