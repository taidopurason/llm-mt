The "loop up" sign looks like the letter V, and the "bow down" looks like a clip or square that is missing the underside.
Top means that you start at the tip and push the bow, bottom means that you start at the flip-flop (where the hand holds the bow) and pull the bow.
Upward movement usually produces a softer sound, while downward movement is stronger and more penetrating.
You can write your own marks on the sheet in pencil, but keep in mind that printed marks have a reason from a musical point of view, so they should usually be followed.
