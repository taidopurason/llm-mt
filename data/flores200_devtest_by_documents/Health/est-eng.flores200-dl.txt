Although one experimental vaccine appears to reduce mortality from Ebola, no drugs have yet been found that are suitable for treating the existing infection.
One antibody cocktail, ZMapp, showed promise in practice at first, but formal trials showed it had less than the desired benefit in preventing death.
In the PALM trial, ZMapp was used as a control - in other words, the researchers used it as the baseline against which they compared the other three treatments.

It has been established that a dead wild duck found in a marsh near Lyon in eastern France on Monday was infected with the H5N1 strain of avian influenza, which is deadly to humans.
France is the seventh country in the European Union to have a problem with the virus, following Austria, Germany, Slovenia, Bulgaria, Greece and Italy.
Suspected cases of H5N1 in Croatia and Denmark have not been confirmed.
