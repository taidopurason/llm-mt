An opinion poll of 1,400 people conducted before the 2010 federal election showed that the number of people opposed to turning Australia into a republic was up 8 percent from 2008.
During the 2010 federal election campaign, Prime Minister Julia Gillard said she believed Australia should become a republic at the end of Queen Elizabeth II's reign.
34 per cent of survey participants shared this view and wanted Queen Elizabeth II to be Australia's last monarch.
In terms of the extreme views within the survey, 29 per cent of respondents believe Australia should become a republic as soon as possible and 31 per cent think Australia should never become a republic.
