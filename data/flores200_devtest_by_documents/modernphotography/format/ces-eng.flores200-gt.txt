For example, the most common photo format in the world is 35mm, the most common size of the film at the peak of an analog films era.
They are still produced today, but more importantly, their aspect ratio has also taken over the formats of digital camera sensors.
Although it is somewhat confusing, the 35mm format actually has a width of 36mm and a height of 24mm.
The ratio of the aspects of this format (divided by twelve to obtain the simplest ratio in the integers) is 3: 2.
Many common formats (for example, APS formats) equal or close to this part of the parties.
