By 17 September 1939, the Polish defence had been broken and the only hope was to retreat and regroup along the Romanian bridgehead.
However, these plans collapsed almost overnight when more than 800 000 Soviet Red Army troops entered and established a front in Belarus and Ukraine after the invasion of eastern Poland, in violation of the Riga Peace Treaty, the Soviet-Polish Non-Aggression Pact and other international treaties, both bilateral and multilateral.
