By September 17, 1939, the Polish defense had already been broken, and the only hope was to retreat and regroup along the Romanian bridgehead.
However, these plans failed almost overnight, when more than 800,000 soldiers of the Red Army of the Soviet Union entered and created the Belarusian and Ukrainian fronts after the conquest of the eastern territories of Poland, in violation of the Riga Peace Treaty, the Soviet-Polish Non-aggression Pact and other international treaties, both bilateral and multilateral.
