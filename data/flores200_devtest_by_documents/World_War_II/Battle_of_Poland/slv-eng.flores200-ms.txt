By 17 September 1939, the Polish defense had already been broken, with the only hope being the withdrawal and redistribution along the Romanian bridgehead.
These plans, however, became obsolete practically overnight, when 800,000 soldiers of the Soviet Union's Red Army marched and established the Belarusian and Ukrainian fronts after the occupation of eastern Polish regions, in contravention of the Riga Peace Treaty, the Soviet-Polish Non-Aggression Pact and other international treaties, both bilateral and multilateral.
