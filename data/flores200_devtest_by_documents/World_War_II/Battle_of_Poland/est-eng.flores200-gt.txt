By September 17, 1939, Poland defense had already been broken, and the only hope was to retreat and group around the Romanian bridge heads.
However, these plans failed almost overnight, when more than 800,000 Soviet Red Army soldiers entered and created the Belarusian and Ukrainian fronts after the conquest of the eastern Poland, violating the Riga Peace Treaty, the Soviet-Polish Non-aggression Pact and other two and multilateral international agreements.
