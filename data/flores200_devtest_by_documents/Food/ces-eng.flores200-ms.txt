German pastries are quite good, and in Bavaria they are rich and diverse, very similar to their southern neighbors in Austria.
Fruit pastries are common. Apples are used in bread all year round, and cherries and plums appear during the summer.
Many German baked products also contain almonds and hazelnuts and other nuts. Popular desserts are often especially suitable for a cup of strong coffee.
If you want to taste some hearty sweets, try the desserts, which are called Berliner, Pfannkuchen or Krapfen, depending on the region.

Curry can be either "dry" or "wet" depending on how much liquid it contains.

With 17,000 islands to choose from, Indonesian food is an umbrella term that covers a wide range of regional cuisines from across the country.
However, when used without further specification, the term usually refers to cuisine originating from the central and eastern part of the island of Java.
Javanese cuisine, now widely available across the archipelago, contains plenty of simply flavoured dishes dominated by peanuts, chilli, sugar (especially Javanese coconut sugar) and various aromatic spices.
