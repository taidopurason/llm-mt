German pastries are quite good, and in Bavaria they are quite rich and varied, similar to their southern neighbour Austria.
Fruit pastries are commonplace, with cooked apples in pastries all year round, but cherries and plums come out in summer.
Almond, hazelnut and other nuts also feature in most German pastries. The popular cakes are usually especially good with a cup of strong coffee.
If you want a pastry that is small but rich, try something called, depending on the region, a Berlin doughnut, pancake or doughnut.
A curry is a dish based on herbs and spices combined with meat or vegetables.
Curries can be either 'dry' or 'wet', depending on the amount of liquid.
In the interior of northern India and Pakistan, yoghurt is often used in curries; in southern India and some other coastal regions of this subcontinent, coconut milk is commonly used.
Indonesian food is the generic name given to the wide variety of local dishes found throughout the country's 17,000 islands.
However, the term, without additional qualifiers, mostly refers to food originating from the central and eastern parts of the main island of Java.
Javanese cuisine, which is now widespread throughout the archipelago, is characterised by a series of simply spiced dishes, with peanuts, chillies, sugar (especially Javanese coconut sugar) and various aromatic spices being the predominant flavours that Javanese love.
