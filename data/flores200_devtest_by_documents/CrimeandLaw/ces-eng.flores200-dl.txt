Both cyanuric acid and melamine have been found in urine samples from domestic animals that died after consuming contaminated feed.
The university researchers said the two compounds react with each other to form crystals that can block kidney function.
The researchers observed crystals formed in the urine of cats by adding melamine and cyanuric acid.
The composition of these crystals, when compared using infrared spectroscopy (FTIR), matched those found in the urine of affected pets.
