Video recording has led to important discoveries in the interpretation of micropositions, facial movements that last only a few milliseconds.
In particular, it is said that by interpreting micro-expressions correctly, it should be possible to detect whether a person is lying.
Oliver Sacks explained in his article 'The President's Speech' that people who cannot understand speech due to brain damage are nevertheless able to accurately assess sincerity.
He even suggests that animals, such as domestic dogs, have the same ability to interpret human behaviour.
