Most observed rhythms in psychology and behavior often depend significantly on the presence of endogenous cycles and their formation through biological clocks.
Repetitive rhythms, other than responses to periodic external stimuli, have been recorded in most living beings, including bacteria, fungi, plants, and animals.
Biological clocks are self-sustaining oscillators that continue periods of continuous cyclic repetitions even in the absence of external stimuli.
