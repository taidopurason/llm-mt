Many observed rhythms in physiology and behavior often depend fundamentally on the presence of endogenous cycles and their production through the biological clock.
Periodic rhythms that are not simply responses to external periodic stimuli have been documented in most living things, including bacteria, fungi, plants, and animals.
Biological clocks are self-contained oscillators that continue to cycle freely in time even in the absence of external stimuli.
