There are many social and political influences, such as the introduction of the metric system, the transition from absolutism to republicanism, nationalism and the belief that the state belongs to the people, not to the sole ruler.
Even after the Revolution, the fields of activity were open to all male candidates, which allowed the most ambitious and successful to achieve success.
The same goes for the military, because instead of the military ranks being based on class, they were now based on military value.
The French Revolution also inspired representatives of the suppressed working class of many other countries to start their own revolution.
