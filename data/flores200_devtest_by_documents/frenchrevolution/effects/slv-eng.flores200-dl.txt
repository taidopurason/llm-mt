There are many social and political effects, such as the use of the metric system, the transition from absolutism to republicanism, nationalism and the belief that the state belongs to the people and not to a single ruler.
In addition, after the Revolution, the professions were open to all male applicants, allowing the most ambitious and successful to succeed.
This is also true of the army, since military ranks are now based on calibre rather than class.
The French Revolution also inspired many other oppressed members of the working class from other countries to start their own revolutions.
