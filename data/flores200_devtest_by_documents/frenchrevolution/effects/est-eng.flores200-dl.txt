There are many social and political influences, such as the introduction of the metric system, the shift from absolutism to liberalism, nationalism and the belief that the state belongs to the people, not to the sole ruler.
Even after the Revolution, the professions were open to all male candidates, allowing the most ambitious and successful to succeed.
The same was true of the military, because instead of being based on class, military ranks were now based on military merit.
The French Revolution also inspired the oppressed working classes of many other countries to launch their own revolutions.
