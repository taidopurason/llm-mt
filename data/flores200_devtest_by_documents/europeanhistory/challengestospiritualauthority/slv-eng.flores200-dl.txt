During this period of European history, the Catholic Church came under scrutiny, enriching itself and expanding its power.
For more than a millennium, the Christian faith united European countries despite differences in language and customs. I
Its omnipresent power influenced kings and common people alike.
One of the main Christian doctrines is that wealth should be used to alleviate suffering and poverty, and that the Church's funds are intended to do just that.
The seat of the Church's central authority has been based in Rome for over a thousand years, and this concentration of power and money has led many to question whether it really follows this principle.
