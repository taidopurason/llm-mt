During this period of European history, the Catholic Church, which had become rich and abnormal, began to be carefully controlled.
For over a thousand years, Christian religion had united European countries, despite the differences between their languages and customs.
Its comprehensive power influenced everyone, from the king to the ordinary citizen.
One of the main beliefs of Christians is that wealth should be used to alleviate suffering and poverty, and the funds of the church are designed for this purpose.
The center of ecclesiastical power had been in Rome for over a thousand years, and the concentration of power and money raised the question of whether this principle was adhered to.
