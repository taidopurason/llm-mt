The Gothic style reached its peak between the 10th and 11th centuries and in the 14th century.
Initially, dress in the East had a strong touch of Byzantine culture.
Due to slow communication connections, however, styles in the West could lag behind from 25 to 30 years.
Toward the end of the Middle Ages, their own style began to be formulated in Western Europe. One of the greatest advances of the time, and as a result of the Crusades, was that they began to use buttons to fasten clothes.
