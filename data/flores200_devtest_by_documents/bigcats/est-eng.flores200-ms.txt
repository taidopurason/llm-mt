In the lower tier of them are medium-sized cats, whose food is medium-sized birds of prey, starting with hares and ending with antelopes and goats.
Lastly, there are many small cats (including stray domestic cats) that eat significantly more small prey, such as insects, rodents, lizards and birds.
The secret to their success is the concept of a niche, the specific work that each cat does that keeps it from competing with others.
