Below them are medium-sized felids whose food consists of medium-sized prey, from rabbits to antelope and deer.
Finally, there are plenty of small cats (including feral domestic cats) whose diet consists mainly of small prey such as insects, rodents, reptiles and birds.
The secret of their success rests on a system of ecological niches, i.e. each cat has its own challenges that do not allow it to compete with the others.
