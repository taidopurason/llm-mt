The militaristic nature of the Roman Empire contributed to medical innovations.
Doctors began to be recruited by the Emperor Augustus, and the first Roman medical corps was even formed to help treat those injured in battle.
Surgeons were familiar with a variety of sedatives, including morphine from poppy seed extract and scopolamine from dog's bane seeds.
They became experts in saving gangrenous patients by amputation and in stopping bleeding with gags and tourniquets.
Over several centuries, the Roman Empire made great advances in medicine and laid the foundations for much of our modern knowledge.
