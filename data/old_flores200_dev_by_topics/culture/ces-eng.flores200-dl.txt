The militaristic nature of the Roman Empire contributed to medical advances.
Emperor Augustus began recruiting doctors and even created the first Roman Medical Corps, which was to find its use after the battles were over.
Doctors were familiar with various sedatives, including morphine from poppy extracts and scopolamine from the seeds of the gourd.
They learned to amputate limbs to save patients from gangrene, and to use tourniquets and arterial clamps to stop the flow of blood.
For several centuries, the Roman Empire made great strides in the field of medicine and shaped much of what we know today.
