"Openness" has also made Estonia a center of attraction for representatives of the dark side.
BNS writes: On Wednesday, the police deported a woman from Brazil who worked as a prostitute in the capital.
According to the Northern Prefecture of the Police and Border Guard Board, the visa-free stay of a 29-year-old Brazilian woman in Estonia was prematurely terminated on Wednesday, November 24, because she provided sexual services for money in Tallinn.
The woman was ordered to leave Estonia and was banned from entering the Schengen territory for one year.
It's not the first time.
In July of this year, the police deported a woman with Columbian citizenship who worked as a prostitute in Estonia.
At that time, the Northern Prefecture conducted illegal proceedings against a Colombian woman born in 1988 who arrived in Estonia with the aim of providing intimate services.
The woman was ordered to leave the country and banned from entering the country for three years.
In December 2019, the portal Uued Uudid wrote: "The police deported a man with Brazilian citizenship who worked as a prostitute in Tallinn.
